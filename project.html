About Me

I create immersive environments with custom instruments, modular systems, and cyclical processing. My work is site-responsive, treating sound, space, and listener as one composition. With over fifteen years of experience in performance and touring, my current focus involves developing portable systems that make spatial sound accessible across diverse environments.

Distributed Listener System

Distributed Listener System is a portable system in development for composing with space. Custom instruments and networked devices listen to the audience and the room, then reshape and redistribute sound in real time to create evolving, site-specific works.

CYCL & NODE-1

Project Overview

CYCL is a self-designed performance synthesizer that captures and transforms sound in real time. NODE-1 is a network of wireless audio processing units, each with its own microphone, DSP, and speaker output. Together, they form a Distributed Listener System where sound moves through physical space.

The controller transmits effect states and spatial movement patterns wirelessly to the Listeners. In direct mode, all Listeners mirror the same processing in real time. In sequencer mode, effects rotate through the Listeners at adjustable tempos, with envelope-based crossfading creating smooth spatial transitions. Sound appears to travel through the room, turning space itself into a performable element.

Each Listeners processes audio independently using custom granular algorithms (pitch shifting, time stretching, grain manipulation). The system uses a decentralized architecture where no central mixer exists, distributing CPU load across Listeners and maintaining true spatial separation.

Enclosures are hand-built from reclaimed and street-found materials. The controller uses an aluminum case with RF-optimized openings for reliable wireless performance.

The system is currently in prototype testing phase, with working hardware and operational sequencing. Next developments focus on expanding spatial movement patterns, refining interaction modes, and preparing for multi-Listener field testing.

To me, technology must always be in service to the music.

Goals

  • Develop distinct sound scenes and interactive processing algorithms.
  • Build and test systems that facilitate musical collaboration.
  • Create experiences where the space and audience become integral to the composition.

Why It Matters

Spatial audio is often confined to specialized venues and research centers. This project breaks down those barriers by creating portable systems that work in any space, from galleries to DIY venues to outdoor sites.

By making immersive audio accessible and releasing all development as open source, I am building tools that artists anywhere can adapt, modify, and expand, giving more people access to experimental spatial composition.
