About Me

I am a composer, musician, and sound artist creating immersive sonic environments through modular systems, custom instruments, and cyclical processing. I develop adaptable, site-responsive installations and performances where sound, space, and listener are inseparable.

CYCL & NODE-1

Project Overview

CYCL is a custom-built performance synthesizer designed to capture and process sound in real time. NODE-1 is a network of portable listening devices that autonomously alter and cycle audio through a space, creating evolving, site-specific sound environments.

Together, they form a scalable and adaptable system capable of spatializing audio in non-traditional spaces. They can capture and process anything in the room, from my own performance to other musicians or the sounds of the audience, making each presentation a collaborative listening experience shaped by those present.

This is an ongoing body of work that I am continuing to expand. Upcoming development focuses on learning to design PCBs, refining hardware and firmware for greater control and more varied sonic states, and exploring new modes of interaction between CYCL and NODE-1 in both large-scale systems and intimate, improvised setups. Future plans include wireless state control to keep the sound network coherent.

Housings are built from recycled and street-found materials. The first CYCL enclosure was constructed from found wood and reclaimed acrylic, giving the instrument a distinct physical presence while reducing waste.

Spatial Nodes: Listening to audience and shaping the space.

Goals

  • Develop additional sound states and optimize the code for stability and flexibility.
  • Improve musical playability, interaction, and overall sound quality.
  • Design and build a PCB so the system is more compact and can fit more easily into found objects.
  • Integrate a wireless network for scalable deployment of NODE-1 units.
  • Experiment with different musicians and instruments within the system.
  • Refine the physical design for durability, portability, and usability.
  • Test the system with live audiences to evaluate engagement and response.

Why It Matters

I wish for immersive, interactive sound to be possible in any space, not only in specialized rooms. This project builds tools that are portable, scalable, and easy to set up so more people can experience spatial sound.

The system can include non‑musicians and non‑technical participants, inviting audiences to shape what they hear. It supports collaboration with other arts, adapts to galleries and found spaces alike, and keeps the focus on curiosity, play, and shared listening.
