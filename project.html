About Me

I create immersive sonic environments with custom instruments, modular systems, and cyclical processing. My work is site responsive. Sound, space, and listener operate as one composition.

Spatial Nodes

Spatial Nodes is a portable system for composing with space. Custom instruments and networked devices listen to the audience and the room, then reshape and redistribute sound in real time to create evolving, site-specific works.

CYCL & NODE-1

Project Overview

CYCL is a self-designed performance synthesizer that captures and transforms sound in real time. NODE-1 is a network of portable listening units that autonomously cycle and process audio throughout a space.

Together, they form a flexible system for spatializing sound in unconventional settings. Both can respond to live performance, interact with other musicians, or reshape ambient audience noise, turning each presentation into a composition that shifts with the presence and movement of listeners.

Enclosures are built from reclaimed and street-found materials.

The next stage will focus on expanding the instrument’s musicality, enabling more textural layers and interaction between sounds, with each composition shaped by those in the space. Custom PCB design, refined hardware and firmware, and new interaction modes will support these developments.

To me, technology must always be in service to the music.

Concept mockup: spatial nodes listen to the audience and shape the room.

Goals

  • Create more musical modes.
  • Collaborate with different musicians and instruments.
  • Test with live audiences and evaluate engagement.

Why It Matters

The goal is to bring spatial audio out of specialized venues and make it accessible in any space, for anyone. This gives more people the chance to experience immersive audio regardless of location or resources.

All tools developed through this project will be released as open source, encouraging others to adapt and experiment.
